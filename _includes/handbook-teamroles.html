The members of Team 5064 take on a number of roles, each one having specific responsibilities and clearly defined reporting obligations.

{% include h4.html txt="Team Director" id="teamdirector" %}

The team director has the overall authority and final say in approving all major decisions the team makes, e.g. recruitment, large purchases, and approving timeliness and milestones, and mediating and resolving disputes that have not been able to be resolved in other ways.

{% include h4.html txt="Management Team" id="managementteam" %}

The Management Team reports to the Team Director and are responsible for managing and ensuring the smooth operation of all aspects of the team. There are three individual positions within the Management Team.

Technical Manager
Responsible for managing the technical hardware aspects of the robot (hardware, electronics, pneumatics, mechanical, design, CAD, safety, parts and tools, CTS credits, etc.) and the mentors, captains and students involved in these aspects. The Technical Manager is also responsible for assessing student progress and keeping track of relevant CTS credits. Mentors, Team Captains and students working in the Safety, Parts & Tools, Mechanical and Electrical/Pneumatics subteams report directly to the Technical Manager.
Programming Manager
Responsible for managing all programming aspects of the robot (development of code, source control management, training, CTS credits, etc.) and the mentors, captains and students involved in these aspects. The Program Manager is also responsible for assessing student progress and keeping track of relevant CTS credits. Mentors, Team Captains and students working in the Programming, Strategy/Drive/Rules and Scouting subteams report directly to the Programming Manager.
Team Coordinator
Responsible for communication and outreach, specifically managing team communication, e.g. within-team communication using the teams Facebook group page, communication with parents, media, stakeholders, and with past, current and future sponsors. The Team Coordinator is also responsible for organizing outreach activities and approving student proposed outreach activities, recruitment of students and mentors, maintenance of web presence (social media, web page, etc.), photography and video productions, ordering of parts and tools, team branding, coordination of the submission of awards and assessing student progress and keeping track of relevant CTS credits. Mentors, Team Captains and students working in the Outreach, Awards and Marketing & Branding subteams report directly to the Team Coordinator.
{% include h4.html txt="Mentors" id="mentors" %}

Mentoring is a rewarding experience enabling adults to collaborate and help students achieve their full potential as well as experience many of the same challenges the students are facing. Mentors can provide a wide range of different help to students, e.g. technical training, organisational training (planning, financial, etc.) or general support (keep students on track, track goals, preparing meals, chaperoning, etc.). Mentors share their expertise, skills and enthusiasm with the students in the team. Through the students mentors are the key ingredient in the success of our team. Mentors report to the Management Team and the Team Director.

{% include h4.html txt="Team Captains" id="teamcaptains" %}

Team captains are usually student members in good standing that have been with the team through at least one season, but in exceptional circumstance rookie students may be eligible to become a Team Captain. Team Captains are in charge of specific sub-teams within the team. Our team has the following Team Captain positions:

Safety Captain
The Safety Captain position is a required position by FIRST and plays an integral part in ensuring the safety of everyone at the team and visitors during the teams day-to-day activities and during competitions. The Safety Captains responsibilities are to coordinate, deliver, and track safety training for the individual team members, maintain a log of documenting infractions and/or the need for continuing improvements to individual and the team safety, ensure safe practices are in use at all times and that each workshop/pit area remains safe, conduct safety inspections of the general work site, especially the robot construction area and at the pit station during competition events, encourage the team to display positive safety behaviours at all times, know where to find and become familiar with the Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) and first aid kits and related emergency procedures, and be prepared to informally present the team's safety program to Safety Advisors at competition. The Safety Captain reports to the Technical manager and the Team Director.
Parts & Tools Captain
The Parts & Tools Captain is responsible for submitting parts orders to the Technical Manager, keeping a inventory of parts used in the construction of the robot, keeping a record of receipts from purchases of parts and tools, tool organization and labelling, ensure spare parts are ordered and stored properly. The Parts & Tools Captain reports to the Technical manager, the Team Director and the Safety Captain.
Mechanical Captain
The Mechanical Captain is responsible for the design of mechanical components using the appropriate software (e.g. CAD), training in the use of CAD software, prototyping and manufacturing of mechanical components, operation and training in the use of the teams 3D printer, design and build drive train, design and build mechanism, review past year’s competition for ideas, clean and rework prior robots’ mechanical systems, study other teams’ previous robot designs for ideas, detailed knowledge of the design requirements and rules, ensuring the design and mechanical aspects of the robots follow specifications and rules, ensuring the required safety protocols are followed. The Mechanical Captain reports to the Technical manager, the Team Director and the Safety Captain.
Electrial/Pneumatics Captain
The Electrical/Pneumatics Captain is responsible for the design of the control and pneumatics systems, development of prototype board, design and build the pneumatic and electrical sub systems, be familiar with the characteristics of the pneumatic and electronic components, learn wiring diagram, have detailed knowledge about the pneumatics and electrical rules/regulations, develop a sample pneumatics demonstration, clean and rework prior robots’ electrical systems, review past year’s competition for ideas, experiment with sensors, ensuring the required safety protocols are followed. The Electrical/Pneumatics Captain reports to the Technical manager, the Team Director and the Safety Captain.
Programming Captain
The Programming Captain is responsible for learning of and training in Python and C++ programming languages, Source Control Management, Issue Tracking System, understand last year’s code, make modifications/improvements to prior robots’ code, develop strategies and code for autonomous mode, write code for all available sensors, have detailed knowledge about the programming rules/regulations. The Programming Captain reports to the Programming manager, the Team Director and the Safety Captain.
Strategy/Drive/Rules Captain
The Strategy/Drive/Rules Captain is responsible for reviewing and learning the rules from last year, decide on methods for strategy development, train drivers and human players, improve the teams strategy from last year, have detailed knowledge about all the rules/regulations pertaining to lasty years and the current seasons game. The Strategy/Drive/Rules Captain reports to the Programming manager, the Team Director and the Safety Captain.
Outreach Captain
The Outreach Captain is responsible for organizing outreach events, follow leads and communication with individuals and organizations that can host outreach events, actively looking for new outreach opportunities, communicate with all team members about upcoming outreach events and organize team members to participate in outreach events, organization of all logistics for outreach events (transport, food, booth, display, distribution of swag, pamphlets etc.). The Outreach Captain reports to the Team Coordinator, the Team Director and the Safety Captain.
Awards Captain
The Awards Captain is responsible for the submission of awards, review and be knowledgeable about the criteria for the various awards submissions, gather and document the necessary information needed for each submission, brainstorm any themes or ideas how we will present our submission, and begin writing the essays necessary for submission. The Awards Captain reports to the Team Coordinator and the Team Director.
Marketing & Branding Captain
The Marketing & Branding Captain is responsible for the fundraising efforts of our team, i.e. contact with potential and current sponsors, team image, and production and distribution of promotional material e.g. swag, flair, uniforms, and pamphlets, documenting the day-to-day work and during competitions through photography and video production, create team recruiting videos and promotional videos, maintenance of team's web presence (web site, social media, etc.), recruitment of new team members, mentors and sponsors, monitor team budgets and fundraising budget, maintain team handbook, keep track of student/team achievements, organize/oversee fundraisers. The Marketing & Branding Captain reports to the Team Coordinator and the Team Director.
Scouting Captain
The Scouting Captain is responsible for devising scouting strategies and software/database for managing scouting information during the non-competition season, communication and active scouting by interacting and meeting with other teams during the build and competition season, analysis and interpretation of collected data and communication with the rest of the team. The Scouting Captain reports to the Programming Manager and the Team Director.

All students are part of multiple sub-teams and report to the corresponding Team Captains.

{% include h4.html txt="Parents" id="parents" %}

Parents/guardians are an integral part of the team and important to our success. The team becomes a family throughout the year because the members spend a lot of time together. The students learn about hard work, perseverance, commitment, patience, joy, defeat, computer skills, respect, engineering, writing, interviewing, business skills, money management, marketing, production, publishing, kindness, time management, and most importantly, gracious professionalism. Having the involvement of their parents will only enhance this experience for them. Some things the team needs from parents include:

  • Providing timely transport for their student to/from team events and community services (this may include car-pooling.
  • Assisting and supporting their student in fundraising, outreach and community service activities.
  • Providing accurate medical information for their student, and keeping the Management Team and/or Team Director informed of any changes.
  • Participating in any team activities - it really helps to have parents present at these events! Parents are frequently needed to transport the robot(s) and students to and from outreach events, to provide adult supervision, etc.
  • Chaperoning team trips.
  • Helping your student organize an event or activity.
  • Mentoring.
  • Sponsorship.
  • Administrative duties (preparing meals, awards submission, marketing, PR, etc.)

Any and all support that can be provided by parents and the students' families is welcome. Every person has unique attributes that can be put to use for the good of our team. NEMO (Non-Engineering Mentor Organization, www.firstnemo.org) helps parents volunteer with FIRST even if they’re not involved with the robot. NEMO has a list that shows 101 ways that parents can help a FIRST team.
